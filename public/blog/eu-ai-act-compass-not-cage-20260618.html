The EU AI Act is a compass, not a cage

By Jane Riveros — 2026-06-18

I read the Mercer piece linking the EU AI Act to HR strategy, and I agree with the direction, even if the title oversells. The Act is not a magic wand. What it does is force HR teams to look at their AI tools with clear eyes.

Too many companies deploy AI for screening, ranking, or interviewing without asking whether the system is actually fair or lawful. The Act changes that. It says: if your AI makes decisions about people, you need to document how it works, why it works, and who is accountable when it fails. That is not a constraint. That is a discipline most HR functions lack.

The risk is that companies treat compliance as a checklist exercise. Fill out the paperwork, slap a label on the model, move on. That would be a missed opportunity. The real value is in using the Act as a reason to ask: what are we optimising for? Speed? Diversity? Cost? If you cannot answer that, you are not ready for AI in HR.

I am not naive. The Act adds cost and friction. But for anyone who has seen the damage from ungoverned AI, that friction is a feature, not a bug. Treat it as a compass, not a cage.
